About Us

The Indian Navy is the maritime arm of the Indian Armed Forces. Its primary role is to safeguard the nation's maritime interests and deter or defeat any threat or aggression at sea. The Indian Navy is also responsible for maritime security and coastal defense.

The Indian Navy operates a wide range of ships and aircraft, including aircraft carriers, cruisers, destroyers, frigates, submarines, and helicopters.

Careers

The Indian Navy offers a variety of career opportunities for both men and women. You can join the Indian Navy as an officer or a sailor. To join as an officer, you must have a bachelor's degree from a recognized university. To join as a sailor, you must have passed Class 12th from a recognized school.

To learn more about the different career opportunities available in the Indian Navy, please visit the Indian Navy website.
